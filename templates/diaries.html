Get Inspired by those who Perspired

Narayan Murthi

Founder of Infosys
Organisations with decision-making speed & imagination will thrive as nobody can claim to have a monopoly over creativity. – N. R. Narayana Murthy
Narayana Murthy is the co-founder of Infosys, a multinational corporation that provides services pertaining to technology, engineering, consulting and outsourcing. Mr. Murthy founded Infosys in 1981. Narayana Murthy did not grow up with the most favourable circumstances but that didnt hold him back from achieving phenomenal success. In 1981, after quitting his job as a General Manager at Patni Computer Systems in Pune, and borrowing Rs 10,000 from his wife, Murthy started Infosys. In a span of only four years, the company grew into a tech giant and employed over 2 lakh people, while also making Bangalore the IT hub of India. Apart from his towering contributions to the Indian economy through his company, Narayana Murthy also made significant benefactions to the IT world. He is now recognised as “the father of the IT sector”.

Ritesh Agarwal

He is the founder and CEO of disruptive hospitality business and app - Oyo Rooms - a network of 2,200 hotels operating in 154 cities across India - with monthly revenues of $3.5m and 1,500 employees. It also features among the companies which are tipped to become the next start-up unicorns according to CB Insights research's findings, published in The New York Times. It has raised a total of $125million of funding in 4 rounds from 7 investors. Agarwal has won many awards and accolades for his work including the Business World Young Entrepreneur Award.

Lakshmi Mittal

Executive Chairman of ArcelorMittal
Chairman of stainless-steel manufacture Aperam
Lakshmi Mittal serves as chairman of $53.3 billion (revenue) ArcelorMittal, the world's largest steel and mining company by output. Since 2008, Mittal was a member of the board of directors of Goldman Sachs. He sits on the World Steel Association’s exclusive committee. Lakshmi Mittal first learned about business from his father, Mohan Lal Mittal. After graduation, Mittal worked for the family firm for a while, and then struck out on his own in 1976 when he founded a steel plant in Indonesia. Calling his steel company Arcelor Mittal, he ran this business successfully throughout the 1980s, and in 1995 Mittal made a full separation of his own steel business from the family's corporate holdings in India. Soon afterward, Mittal settled in London, where he still resides to this day. Mittal, an accountants with a global vision and practically endless determination to succeed, overcame obstacles and prevailed in consolidating the extensively divided steel industry. At 71 (2021) years of age, Mittal could certainly be considered the Andrew Carnegie of the new millennium.

Radhakishan Damani

DMart founder and owner
Radha Kishan Damani, is India’s 11th richest person, an entrepreneur, a stock Market investor and the owner of D-mart. Just a 12th pass man, he built an empire of $10bn. Born in Bikaner, Rajasthan in a Marwari family in 1960, Damani’s father ran a small business. His untimely death led him to start a small business of ball bearings, despite having no practical knowledge of business and stock market. He just scrutinized and reviewed the stock market in his initial days and learned ample valuable lessons. He made a profit by trading and investing in MNC companies and began investing in the stock market at the age of 32. Radha Kishan is inspired by a value investor named Chandra Kant Sampat. He followed the strategy of value investing and long term investing from Mr. Sampat. He used to purchase the stocks at a lower price, keep it for longer periods and then sell it. He became the biggest individual shareholder of HDFC in the year 1995. Damani has exemplified that determination, passion, farsightedness, confidence, and knowledge can beat all the qualifications of the world.

Indra Nooyi

Chairman of PepsiCo
The lady with a fizz, Indra Krishnamurthy Nooyi was the chairman and CEO of PepsiCo, the world's fourth-largest food and Beverage Company for 12 Years. Indra Nooyi has stepped down after 12 years as CEO, President Ramon Laguarta to succeed her. An Indian-born American executive, Nooyi was ranked No.4 on Forbes magazine's annual survey of the 100 most powerful women in the world. Prior to becoming CEO she was the President, Chief Financial Officer and a member of the Board of Directors of PepsiCo Inc. Now She is serving as Board of Director of Amazon. She has also been awarded Padma Bhushan, CNN-IBN Indian of the Year Global Indian.

Deep Kalra

founder and group CEO of MakeMyTrip
Deep Kalra obtained his bachelor’s degree from St. Stephen’s College, Delhi and his Master of Business Administration degree (MBA) degree from IIM Ahmedabad. He went on to work for companies like ABN AMRO Bank, GE Capital, At GE Money etc. Three years at the bank, Deep Kalra took a huge risk by quitting the good paying and stable job at ABN Bank and deciding to turn an entrepreneur. He brought the AMF Bowling – an American company that was hoping to enter the Indian market to start the 10-pin bowling revolution in the country. However, the venture failed, and he went back to a boring and regular private job. He thinks that failure in his case helped him a lot to keep going. Hence, with a backing of e-Ventures and along with co-founders, Deep began his new Entrepreneurial journey with an online travel company, MakeMyTrip after realizing the possibilities of the internet. MakeMyTrip is now the largest Indian online travel company which provides online travel services like hotel reservations, Air tickets, domestic and international holiday packages, Cab Booking, train booking and bus tickets, etc.

Hari Menon

Big Basket CEO & Co-Founder
Hari Menon was born in 1996 in Bandra West, Mumbai into a middle-class family. Hari is the CEO and Co-Founder of BigBasket, India’s largest supermarket. He is also a Founder of Symphony Industrial AI. Before founding Big Basket, Hari served as the CEO for various companies including Tumri, Serus Corporation, and Timeli. In 1999, Hari along with five of his friends, the founding team of BigBasket had cofounded Fabmart, an online platform, which performed the same functions that Amazon, Flipkart does today and was one of the pioneers in e-commerce in India. During the first year of his online platform, there were no transactions but only noise, as it could not generate enough revenue. After the failure of Fabmart they launched Fabmall, a physical version of Fabmart and India’s first integrated online-to-offline retail company, a venture which took off. Around the same time, Hari Menon decided to sell- off the business to Aditya Birla Group. After the success of Fabmart, Hari Menon, Vipul Parekh, V S Sudhakar, Abhinay Choudhari, and V S Ramesh founded Bigbasket in October 2011. Hari Menon is one of those Indian entrepreneurs, who struggle a lot in their initial life, entrepreneurial journey, and then step onto the market with a unique business idea and get success by his pivotal efforts.

Azim Premji

Chairman of Wipro Limited
Indian entrepreneur Azim Premji turned a small, family-owned cooking-oil company into Wipro Limited, a multinational conglomerate focused on providing technology outsourcing services, that many have compared to Microsoft. In 1980, Premji seized the opportunity to transform Wipro into an IT company when IBM withdrew its business from India. Premji is sometimes referred to as India's Bill Gates as a result, alongside his informal title as "czar" of the Indian IT industry. In 2010, he was voted among the 20 most powerful men in the world by Asiaweek. He has twice been listed among the 100 most influential people by Time magazine, once in 2004 and more recently in 2011. For years, he has been regularly listed one among the 500 Most Influential Muslims.
